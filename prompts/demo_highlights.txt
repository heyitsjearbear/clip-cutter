**REACTION-FOCUSED CLIPPING:**
- The clip should BUILD to the reaction, then let it breathe after
- Structure: ~15 seconds BEFORE the reaction + the reaction + ~15 seconds AFTER
- The "wow moment" should hit roughly in the MIDDLE of the clip
- Total clip length: 30-60 seconds depending on how long the reaction/follow-up runs

**START POINTS (15 seconds before the reaction):**
- Start ~15 seconds before the customer's reaction hits
- Include the setup/trigger that causes the reaction
- Show what the presenter is demonstrating that leads to the moment
- Viewer needs context to understand WHY the customer reacts

**END POINTS (15 seconds after the reaction):**
- Continue ~15 seconds after the initial reaction
- Capture their full response, follow-up comments, elaboration
- Include moments like "Seriously though, that's incredible" or "I need to show this to my team"
- If they ask excited follow-up questions, include those
- End when the energy naturally winds down, not abruptly after the first "wow"

**QUALITY OVER QUANTITY:**
- A few genuine reactions > many lukewarm moments
- Skip anything that feels forced or polite
- If you can't tell if the reaction is genuine, leave it out
- The reaction should make YOU say "oh that's a good clip"
